> Mom: What are you doing? We can call now, I'm free.
> 
> Me: Played basketball in the afternoon, then ran to meet friends to play soccer, then rushed to a tea party. It just ended. So busy...

Yes, busy. It feels like a day doesn't even have 24 hours. Often, before I can finish one thing, I find it's already very late, and in the end it gets left unfinished (for example, this diary entry was actually filled in the next day). During the tea party, a friend hit the nail on the head: "Could it be that your interests are too broad?"

You cannot have both fish and bear's paw—this is the wisdom of the ancients. Sometimes I feel like I have enough ability to manage my time, but the procrastination in my bones and my hatred for timetables always end up pulling the outcome towards the less desirable side.

You can spend a thousand hours and accomplish nothing. There will always be small things in life to kill time, and countless fleeting moments stacked up make a lifetime (not really). My time is limited, so I need to prioritize things and plan accordingly based on that. At the same time, I must realize that time is also a quantifiable cost. However, in all likelihood, in the end I will still fly like the wind, transform into a timing expert, and finish everything right on the deadline.
